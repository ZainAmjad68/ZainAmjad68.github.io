From a young age, my curiosity for technology defied my non-technical upbringing. This curiosity is what led me to start operating a PC on my own since i was 3. By the age 12, i was the dedicated technophile for the whole block that i lived in. From coding small scripts to automate tasks, to cracking pirated games, i did it all. Hence, it only made sense for me to choose CS as my major once the time came. However, transitioning from a self-taught technophile to a university student proved to be a challenge. I had to adapt to a new learning environment, work with peers from diverse backgrounds, and adjust to a structured curriculum. The first few years of my CS program were challenging, but I was determined to succeed. I spent countless hours studying and working on my assignments, and gradually, my hard work paid off. I found my footing soon enough and started excelling in all the technical subjects. I even took some classes with Masters students because Bachelors didn't feel challenging enough.
